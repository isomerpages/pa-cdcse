<p>Over the years, Contact South East has shared stories of the people, partnerships
and everyday efforts that shape our district to where it is today.</p>
<p>This issue marks a notable transition. Responding to feedback from our
residents, we are shifting Contact South East from its traditional print
format to a hybrid approach. Going forward, Contact South East will have
two digital issues and a special edition in both print and digital formats
to wrap up each year. This transition will allow us to reduce our carbon
footprint while ensuring that we continue to stay connected with our residents
and share the latest happenings in the community.</p>
<p>While we are making this shift, our commitment to supporting the community
remains unchanged.</p>
<p>This includes championing healthier lifestyles, creating opportunities
for our youth, engaging our volunteers’ capabilities and recognising their
contributions, and strengthening partnerships with community and corporate
organisations. Over the last six months or so, I have been heartened by
the passion and commitment of volunteers, partners and community groups
who continue to step forward to meet diverse needs across our district.</p>
<p>In this issue, you will discover efforts to promote community wellness
through programmes such as FairPrice Group Walk for Health @ South East,
and read about dedicated volunteers who form the backbone of many initiatives.</p>
<p>You will also see how we are working with partners to support our youth
through platforms like Career Launch @ South East and Football Clinic @
South East.</p>
<p>As we look ahead, my team and I remain committed to making South East
District more inclusive, resilient and connected. Every programme, partnership
and small act of care</p>
<p>contributes to this shared goal. I thank you for being part of this journey,
and I look forward to staying connected with you through both our digital
platforms and our publications.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
